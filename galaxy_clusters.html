Galaxy Clusters

The main component of my thesis research is studying the mass content of galaxy clusters. This can be done primarily in two ways: gravitational lensing and galaxy dynamics. These two methods come with many assumptions (e.g., clusters are in hydrostatic equilibirum), however, in my thesis I am aiming to combine these methods to minimize the affects of these biases on scientific results. In particular, I aim to better understand the nautre of dark matter by combining these methodlogies.

To begin to chip away at this seemingly intractable problem, I am first investigating various methods of identifying galaxy cluster substructure using galaxy-member dynamics. This is of great interst as different models of dark matter predict different substructure masses as well as varying amounts of substructure. By being able to properly measure such substructures in observations, I can place constraints on what dark matter can actually be made of. But first, we must develop these methods using the IllustrisTNG simulation (pictured above), which allows me to test if the methods I am using are properly tracing the movement of galaxies inside the cluster. Properly tracing the movement of galaxies inside of clusters will enable me to find the galaxy cluster substructure. You can learn more about this research at the project's GitHub page.
